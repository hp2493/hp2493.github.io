Background

South Dakota

As a whole, counties depicted in the South Dakota Opportunity Atlas have high household income outcomes for low income families. However a few counties stand out as having comparatively low household income outcomes for low income families. To learn more about why this might be the case, I compared Oglala Lakota County, which has the lowest outcome for household income, and Harding County, which has the highest outcome. Low income children who grow up in Oglala Lakota County earn an average of $15,000 per year. Low income children who grow up in Harding County earn almost five times that amount at $71,000.

Comparisons


Outcomes

The outcomes for low income children who grow up in Oglala Lakota County vary from those of children who grow up in Harding County. Children who grew up in Oglala Lakota County have an average household income of $15,000 per year compared to Harding County's $71,000 per year.


Source: Opportunity Atlas


Characteristics

To find out more about why the outcomes vary between the two counties, one can look to the characteristics of each county. The average household income for residents of Oglala Lakota County (2012 - 2016) is $26,000 per year compared to Harding County's $51,000 per year.


Source: Opportunity Atlas


Why?

One of the largest differences between the two county characteristics is percent white. When I looked further into the counties to try and explain this difference, I learned that Oglala Lakota County houses the Pine Ridge Reservation, home of the Oglala Sioux Tribe. Many of the reservations in South Dakota struggle with poverty and substance abuse, and much more research is merited on this subject to understand the causes and possible solutions.
